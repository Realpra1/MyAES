FAQ:

Q: What is the init vector?

A: The init vector must be unique for each password or AES will NOT be secure. To ensure this we hash the public address to generate it (as simple ASCII input).

If your encryption was done without this leave the "Address" field blank and IV 000.. will be used.

Q: What is padding, NoPad and PKCS7 padding?

A: AES works with blocks of a certain size (128 bits or 32 bytes and keys of 128, 192 or 256 bits), when input does not match the block length padding is used.

If N bytes are missing to make a full block PKCS7 padding adds N bytes all equal to N. Very simple. If no padding is used input of exactly 32 hexadecimal increments must be used.

You can use NoPad for standard classic length BCH and BTC keys.

Q: Why not just use default google crypto settings or some other online generator?

A: These are the default settings for AES.

Having all algorithmic parameters known and well defined lets you get back your Bitcoin keys without trouble.

Q: Is this secure enough?

A: AES with the parameters used here is the gold standard of cryptography and is believed to be fully secure.

Q: Should I worry about quantum computers breaking cryptography?

A: Perhaps some day, as of 2024 the largest factored prime by a quantum computer is 15. Maintaining proper entanglement of many qbits is extremely hard and maybe practically impossible.
